Human-Centered Design & Clinical Translation

We draw on human-centered computing approaches to develop systems for the translation of imaging-based machine-learning and AI approaches into clinical settings. Specifically, we design, develop, and evaluate systems that embed outcomes of prediction models and AI-based computations in accessible user interfaces so that they can then be integrated into existing clinical workflows. In addition, we develop methods that utilize visual representations to explain predictions to clinicians. We have created computational infrastructure to facilitate the design and development of such systems and for running realistic user studies to evaluate the systems. Current biomedical applications include the clinical translation of automated landmarking for 3D photograms for assessing craniosynostosis, and studying the impact of AI-based decision support on clinical experts' radiographic interpretation of a pediatric brain tumor, adamantinomatous craniopharyngioma.
